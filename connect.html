The Hush Room was not built to be a solo endeavour. It was built to be a community — of churches, partners, volunteers and supporters who believe that an entire generation deserves to be invited into the silence. Here is how you can be part of it.

Our Partnership Vision
"Two are better than one, because they have a good return for their labour: If either of them falls down, one can help the other up."
— Ecclesiastes 4:9-10

Every retreat THR runs requires a venue, a guide, materials, meals — and often a scholarship for someone who could not otherwise come. When you partner with THR — as a church, a volunteer or a financial supporter — you are directly placing a young person in the room where God meets them.

For Churches & Communities

Bring THR to your community.

"We don't replace your church. We resource it — with a practice your young people have never been given space to discover."

Many churches want to offer deeper formation to their youth but lack the trained guides, the physical space, or the framework to do it well. THR partners with churches to bring the silence to their community — either by hosting a retreat at our venue, or by coming to yours.

"Let the children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of God belongs to such as these."
— Mark 10:14 · The founding mandate of THR

The Scholarship Fund

No one turned away for lack of funds.

"Is not this the kind of fasting I have chosen: to loose the chains of injustice and untie the cords of the yoke, to set the oppressed free?" — Isaiah 58:6

The silence should not be a privilege of the affluent. From the beginning THR has committed that financial cost would never be the reason a young person couldn't enter the room. The Scholarship Fund makes this possible.

Every dollar given to the fund directly pays for a retreat place for someone who could not otherwise afford it. No administrative overhead. No marketing spend. Directly — person to retreat.

"Freely you have received; freely give."
— Matthew 10:8

Serve With Us

Give your gifts to the silence.

Every retreat requires a team. Not a large team — but a faithful one. If you have been through THR and want to give back, or if you simply feel called to serve, here is where we need you.

I
Prayer Partners

Every retreat is covered in prayer before, during and after. Prayer partners commit to interceding for each retreat — for the retreatants, the guide, the space and the encounter.

Commit to praying for one retreat per quarter
Receive the retreat date and participant count
No travel required — pray from wherever you are
II
Hospitality Team

The retreat experience begins the moment someone arrives. The hospitality team creates the environment — setting up, preparing food, welcoming, and holding the practical space so the guide can focus on the spiritual direction.

Help set up and pack down retreat venues
Prepare and serve simple, nourishing meals
Warm, quiet, unhurried presence required
III
Spiritual Direction Apprentices

If you are training in spiritual direction, or are a mature Christian with a pastoral heart, we welcome direction apprentices who learn by observing and then gradually practising under supervision.

Prior faith maturity and pastoral sensitivity required
Commitment to your own formation and supervision
Interview and assessment required before placement
IV
Creative & Digital

Photography, videography, graphic design, social media — helping THR tell its story with the same beauty and intentionality as the retreats themselves.

Photographers — retreat documentation (sensitively handled)
Designers — print materials, journal covers, social content
Writers — testimonial editing and resource writing
V
Administration & Operations

Behind every retreat is logistics, communication, registration management and financial administration. The unseen team that makes the visible work possible.

Registration management and participant communication
Venue booking and logistics coordination
Finance and scholarship fund administration
VI
Intercessory Community

A committed community of intercessors who pray regularly for the ministry as a whole — for provision, for wisdom, for the young people who have not yet found their way through the door.

Monthly prayer updates and specific requests
Quarterly prayer gathering (in person or online)
A willingness to carry this ministry in prayer
